Discovered by / at:

Towako Hiramatsu,2025-during an underwater light experiment

Family:

Hanabii belongs to a rare family of bioluminescent sea spirits. It was first seen deep underwater, where almost no light reaches

Habitat:

Hanabii lives in dark, quiet parts under the water. It hides near the seafloor and comes out only when it feels energy or light nearby.

Appearance:

Hanabii has a soft pink body and two bright eyes that glow in the dark.It carries a small fireball in its hand that flickers like a candle.When Hanabii feels happy, fireworks burst around it, lighting up the sea with many colors.

Key Characteristics:

  • Glow when it feels energy
  • Can create fireworks underwater
  • Fire in its hand moves like it's alive
  • Curious about human and light
  • ...

What we know so far about [ Hanabii ]? Hanabii was born in the dark ocean and lived alone for a long time. One day, it touched a small spark from a human experiment. That spark made Hanabii shine for the first time, filling its body with bright colors. Since then, it learned to use light to express feelings — joy, curiosity, and hope. Every time Hanabii shines, the ocean around it becomes more alive, as if celebrating with it.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature Hanabii lives in the water and loves calm light.In the daytime, click to make bubbles rise and seaweed move.At night, press a key to turn the world dark and bright.Click to make colorful fireworks appear — Hanabii feels happy and alive.Be gentle and patient.
